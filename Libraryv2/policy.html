Policy and Procedures for the Library Management System

1. User accounts

Users must create a user account on the Library Management System in order to borrow books from the library.

Users must provide their name, email, and contact number when creating a user account, and must verify their email address to ensure it is valid and that they are the owner of the email address.

Users must keep their personal information up-to-date, and must inform the library if any of their information changes.

Users must keep their password confidential and must not share it with anyone else.

2. Use of the system

Users must not attempt to access another user's account or to manipulate the system in any way that violates the library's policies or the law.

3. Borrowing books

Users must borrow books according to the library's borrowing rules and limits, and must return books on time to avoid overdue fees.

Users must treat the books with care and respect, and must not damage or deface them in any way.

Users must report any lost or damaged books to the library as soon as possible, and must pay any applicable fees or replacement costs.

4. User feedback

Users may provide feedback about the system, the books, or other aspects of the library, and the library may use this feedback to improve the system and the services it offers.

5. Modification and termination

The library reserves the right to modify or terminate the Library Management System, or any part of it, at any time and without notice.

6. Suspension and termination of user accounts

Policy for Suspension and Termination of User Accounts

The Library Management System is provided to users for the purpose of borrowing books from the library. By creating a user account on the system, you agree to use the system in accordance with these terms and conditions and the library's policies.

If you violate these terms and conditions or the library's policies, the library reserves the right to suspend or terminate your user account, or to restrict your access to the Library Management System. This may occur, for example, if you:

  • Provide false or misleading information when creating your user account
  • Share your password with another person or allow another person to use your user account
  • Access another user's account without their permission or attempt to manipulate the system in any unauthorized way
  • Borrow books without complying with the library's borrowing rules and limits, or fail to return borrowed books on time
  • Damage or deface books, or fail to report lost or damaged books to the library

If the library suspends or terminates your user account, you will be notified by email or other means, and your access to the Library Management System will be restricted accordingly. The library's decision to suspend or terminate your user account will be final, and no appeals or exceptions
